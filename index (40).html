📐 Months of Stock (MOS) by Plant
Stock-on-hand ÷ consumption, per plant. HO01 is the distribution hub — it has no consumption of its own, so its MOS is calculated against total branch demand.
Upload AMC.xlsx using the 📐 Upload AMC.xlsx button in the sidebar to enable MOS analysis.
ℹ️ HO01 (Hub) MOS rule: HO01 distributes stock to all branch plants and does not consume it directly. For HO01, MOS = HO01's stock-on-hand ÷ the sum of every branch plant's AMC for that item — i.e. how many months HO01 can keep supplying the entire network at current demand. For every other plant, MOS = that plant's stock-on-hand ÷ its own AMC.

🧯 Overstock & Expiry Risk Analysis
Stock that will outlive its shelf life before it can be consumed — with a redistribution plan, and what's left for private sale afterward
Upload the main inventory Excel and AMC.xlsx (📐 AMC upload in the sidebar) to enable this analysis.
ℹ️ How risk is measured: for each plant + item, Months of Stock (MOS) = stock-on-hand ÷ AMC (HO01 uses total branch demand — see MOS by Plant). Shelf-life remaining = months until the earliest-expiring batch at that plant expires. An item is at risk when MOS > shelf-life-remaining — there's more stock than can be sold/used before it goes out of date. The at-risk quantity is only the excess: stock-on-hand minus what could realistically be consumed in time (shelf-life-remaining × AMC).
📉 Expiry Risk Before Redistribution
⚠️ Stock is moved from at-risk plants (including HO01) to plants that can use it before their own shelf-life runs out. HO01 never receives stock in this plan — it's the source, never the destination.
Stock that has nowhere to go in the network before it expires — no plant could absorb it without becoming at-risk. Candidate for private sale, discount channels, or donation.
